I'm Merve (pronounced 'Mehr-veh'), a Master's student in Computational Biology & Bioinformatics at ETH Zürich. I started this page when I was a cancer researcher living in Boston, Massachusetts, to document my journey of learning computational tools and methods as a wet-lab biologist. Now, I am improving myself further during my Master's studies and falling in love with bioinformatics even more.

In my free time, I love to read, write, paint, and learn new languages! 📚🎨

education

Born and raised in Istanbul, Turkey, I graduated from high school in 2019 and moved to the United States for my undergraduate studies. I studied Molecular & Cell Biology with a concentration in Immunology. Having always had a particular interest in cancer, I learned about the immune system, cancer, and how tumors interact with/shape the immune microenvironments around them. Currently, I am pursuing my Master's studies in Computational Biology and Bioinformatics at ETH Zürich in Zürich, Switzerland.

research

I have had many opportunities to get involved in research across different labs, institutes and countries, over the years.

In the second year of my undergraduate studies, I joined the lab of Michel DuPage, where I embarked on my cancer research journey. In the DuPage Lab I worked at the intersection of cancer biology, immunology and epigenetics, to explore ways to selectively reprogram regulatory T cells to enhance anti-tumor immunity while eliminating risk for autoimmune toxicity. I wrote and defended my undergraduate honors thesis about the role of EN101, a cysteine-reactive compound, on Foxp3 regulation in Tregs.

In 2023, I graduated from UC Berkeley and moved to Boston to start working at the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute. Here, I joined the lab of Birgit Knoechel, and was introduced to the exciting world of cutting edge single cell sequencing technologies and bioinformatics. I gained experience in single cell RNA-sequencing, particularly in Chromium Single Cell Gene Expression, Cell Surface Protein Expression and TCR/BCR analysis via 10X genomics. In addition to scRNA-seq, I also worked on a CAR T-cell therapy project with a special focus on preventing resistance.

In 2024, I also started working in the Bandopadhayay Lab at the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute. Here, I worked on pediatric low grade glioma, the most common brain and CNS tumors in children.

At ETH Zürich, as a Master's student, I am currently involved in research projects in different groups. This is my first time working on projects that are entirely computational, a big change from my previous wet-lab driven projects. I have a special interest in and am focusing on the applications of machine learning in different biological contexts related to cancer and infectious diseases.
