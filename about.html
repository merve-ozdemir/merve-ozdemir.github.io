Education

I was born and raised in Istanbul, Turkey, where I completed my primary, secondary and high school education. In 2019, after my high school graduation, I moved to the United States to pursue my undergraduate studies at University of California, Berkeley. At UC Berkeley I studied Molecular & Cell Biology with a concentration in Immunology — a choice driven by my long-standing interest in cancer and infectious diseases.

In 2023 I graduated from UC Berkeley with a simultaneous (double) degree in Molecular & Cell Biology and in Comparative Literature, as well as a minor in French literature. I completed both programs with honors.

After graduating from Berkeley I moved to Boston to work as a Research Technician at the Dana-Farber Cancer Institute. See research experience below.

I am now completing my Master's degree in Computational Biology and Bioinformatics at ETH Zürich in Zürich, Switzerland.

Research

I have had many opportunities to get involved in research across different labs, institutes and countries over the years. Check out the "Publications" tab to see a list of published work I was involved in.

  • 2020 — 2023
    UC Berkeley

    Reprogramming regulatory T cells to enhance anti-tumor immunity DuPage Lab, UC Berkeley

    My first research experience was in the DuPage Lab where I spent 2.5 years working at the intersection of cancer biology, immunology and epigenetics, looking for ways to selectively reprogram regulatory T cells without incurring autoimmune toxicity. My honors thesis examined the effect of EN101, a cysteine-reactive compound, on Foxp3 regulation in Tregs.

  • 2023 — 2024
    Boston, MA

    Single-cell sequencing and CAR T-cell resistance Knoechel Lab, Dana-Farber Cancer Institute

    In my first year at DFCI, in the Knoechel Lab, I was introduced to single-cell sequencing and bioinformatics. I worked across 10x Genomics Chromium single-cell gene expression, cell surface protein expression, and TCR/BCR analyses, alongside a CAR T-cell therapy project focused on preventing resistance.

  • 2024 — 2025
    Boston, MA

    Molecular vulnerabilities & Drug delivery in pediatric gliomas Bandopadhayay Lab, Dana-Farber Cancer Institute

    In my second year at DFCI I joined the Bandopadhayay Lab, where I worked on a project characterizing how a BRAF fusion partner drives oncogenesis in pediatric low grade glioma -- the most common pediatric brain tumor. Here, I also worked on a second project to identify biologic regulators of nanoparticle uptake in diffuse midline glioma.

  • 2025 — 2026 (6 months)
    Zürich, CH

    Machine learning for cancer and infectious disease Reddy Lab, ETH Zürich

    My first fully computational project, after years of wet-lab work, was in the Reddy Lab, where I worked on applying machine learning methods to characterize the TCM-TEM gradient in CAR T Cells using a CD3ζ ITAM mutagenesis library.

I am continuing to work on different research projects as part of my Master's studies. After finishing my Master's thesis, I want to pursue a PhD to continue my learning journey.
